About This Analysis

The 7-Pillar Continuity Analysis evaluates organizational resilience across seven critical dimensions. Each pillar is assessed on a 0-100 scale, with scores aggregated from multiple sub-categories. The overall score of 87 indicates strong continuity capabilities with specific areas requiring attention.

How often should this analysis be updated?
This analysis should be reviewed quarterly and fully updated annually, or whenever there are significant changes to organizational structure, operations, or external threats.
What constitutes a critical gap?
A critical gap is any area scoring below 50% or any single point of failure that could significantly impact organizational continuity within 72 hours.
How are pillar scores calculated?
Each pillar score is a weighted average of its sub-category scores. The weights are based on the relative importance of each sub-category to the overall pillar's continuity objectives.
